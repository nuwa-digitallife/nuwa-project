## 1. APPTRONIK (Austin, TX, USA)

### Company Overview
- **Founded**: 2016
- **CEO**: Jeff Cardenas
- **HQ**: Austin, Texas, USA
- **Status**: Private, heavily funded robotics startup

### Apollo Humanoid Robot - Specifications
| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Height** | 1.7 meters (5'7") |
| **Weight** | 73 kg (161 lbs) |
| **Max Payload** | 25 kg |
| **Battery Runtime** | ~4 hours (hot-swappable) |

| **Market Focus** | Industrial/logistics applications |

### NASA Heritage & Valkyrie Robot Connection
- **Founding Team Background**: Core team worked on NASA's Valkyrie robot (R5)
  - Built by NASA Johnson Space Center with University of Texas and Texas A&M
  - Created for 2013 DARPA Robotics Challenge
  - Dr. Paine (founder) did his Ph.D. research on robotic actuation at UT Austin under Prof. Luis Sentis
- **Legacy**: Apollo is culmination of nearly a decade of development, building on 15+ previous robot iterations
- **Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Contract**: NASA engaged Apptronik to develop liquid-cooled robotic actuator technology

**Sources**:

## 2. 1X TECHNOLOGIES (Formerly Halodi Robotics, Norway)

### Company Overview
- **Former Name**: Halodi Robotics
- **Current Name**: 1X Technologies
- **Founded**: ~2014
- **Original HQ**: Moss, Norway
- **New Global HQ**: Palo Alto, California (opened July 2025)
- **Status**: Private, well-funded robotics startup
- **Headquarters Move**: Consolidated from Moss (Norway) + Sunnyvale (CA) → Palo Alto, 80,000 sq ft facility (400 person capacity)

**Source**: [1X - Palo Alto HQ announcement](https://www.1x.tech/discover/palo-alto-hq)

### NEO Humanoid Robot - Consumer Focus

#### Specifications
| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Height** | 1.65 meters (5'5") |
| **Weight** | 66 pounds (29.94 kg) |
| **Lifting Capacity** | 150+ pounds (68 kg) |
| **Carrying Capacity** | 55 pounds (24.95 kg) |
| **Hand Dexterity** | 22 degrees of freedom |
| **Noise Level** | 22 dB (quieter than refrigerator) |
| **Body Material** | Soft body with custom 3D lattice polymer |
| **Sensors** | WiFi, Bluetooth, 5G, three-stage speaker |
| **Gait** | Realistic bipedal gait, can squat, sit on chairs |

#### Pricing & Availability
- **Early Access Price**: $20,000 (October 2025 pre-order)
- **Monthly Lease Option**: $499/month (6-month minimum)
- **US Shipments Begin**: 2026
- **Target Delivery**: Q3-Q4 2026 (US & Canada)
- **International**: 2027+
- **Market Target**: Consumer/home use (not industrial)

#### Important Note on Autonomy
- NOT fully autonomous in 2026
- Requires teleoperator support for complex tasks
- Platform marketed as "developing" rather than complete

**Sources**:
- [1X NEO specs (BotInfo)](https://botinfo.ai/articles/1x-neo-home-robot)
- [1X Official - NEO ordering page](https://www.1x.tech/discover/neo-home-robot)
- [Origin of Bots review](https://www.originofbots.com/robot/neo-gamma-by-1x-technologies-details-specifications-rating)

### NEO Development Timeline
- **August 30, 2024**: NEO Beta unveiled
- **Beta Deployment**: Limited units deployed to selected homes for R&D
- **February 2025**: NEO Gamma introduced (refinement of Beta)
  - Incorporates feedback from first-wave deployments
  - Improved manufacturability and reliability
  - Closer to consumer readiness

**Source**: [The Robot Report - NEO Beta announcement](https://www.therobotreport.com/1x-unveils-neo-beta-as-it-prepares-to-deploy-into-home-pilots/)

### NEO Beta Deployment Strategy
- **Approach**: Consumer-first strategy (unlike competitors in industrial settings)
- **Rationale**: Homes offer best environment for training general-purpose embodied AI
- **Data Flywheel**: Continuous learning from unstructured, dynamic home interactions
- **Current Scale**: Limited home pilots; data collection phase

**Source**: [Contrary Research - 1X Business Breakdown](https://research.contrary.com/company/1x)

### EVE Wheeled Robot - Industrial Deployment

#### Specifications
| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Height** | 6'2" (1.88m) |
| **Weight** | 192 pounds (87 kg) |
| **Max Speed** | 9 mph (14.5 km/h) |
| **Load Capacity** | 33 pounds (15 kg) |
| **Runtime** | 6 hours |
| **Terrain** | Multi-terrain wheels, can navigate corners and take elevators |
| **Form Factor** | Humanoid torso on wheeled base (Segway-like) |

#### Deployment
- **Current Scale**: 50+ EVE robots actively deployed in 1X's warehouse in Norway
- **Operating Hours**: 8-16 hours daily
- **Purpose**: Task execution and AI data collection
- **Markets**: Logistics, security, healthcare, institutional use

**Source**: [QVIRO - EVE specifications](https://qviro.com/product/1x/eve/specifications)

### NEO vs. EVE Strategy Comparison
- **EVE (Wheeled)**: Commercial/industrial deployment → AI training data collection
- **NEO (Bipedal)**: Consumer/home deployment → embodied AI training in unstructured environments
- **Key Difference**: NEO's two legs vs EVE's wheels critical for home use (small footprint needed for household spaces where wheeled platform wouldn't fit)
- **Phased Approach**: Commercial EVE deployment first to train AI, then consumer NEO launch

**Source**: [Interesting Engineering - EVE task chaining](https://interestingengineering.com/innovation/1x-robotics-voice-task-sequencing)
